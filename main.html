Biography

I am a postdoctoral research fellow specializing in theoretical astrophysics and cosmology. My work seeks to unravel the mysteries of the cosmos, from the dynamics of supermassive black holes to the large-scale structure of the universe. I earned my Ph.D. in Astrophysics from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), focusing on simulations of galaxy formation.

At the Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics, I am currently developing new models to understand gravitational waves and their role as messengers from the early universe. By combining analytical theory with high-performance computing, I aim to push the boundaries of our cosmic understanding.

Research Interests

Black Hole Dynamics

Investigating the mergers of supermassive black holes and their resulting gravitational wave signatures using numerical relativity.

Galaxy Evolution

Simulating the formation and evolution of galaxies across cosmic time to understand their structure and stellar populations.

Exoplanetary Science

Characterizing the atmospheres of distant exoplanets to search for biomarkers and understand planetary formation.
